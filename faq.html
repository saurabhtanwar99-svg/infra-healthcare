Knowledge Hub & FAQs

Expert answers to complex healthcare infrastructure questions.

How does Infra-Healthcare ensure NABH compliance during renovation?
Compliance is integrated at the **blueprint stage**. We strictly follow the National Accreditation Board for Hospitals (NABH) guidelines regarding corridor widths, fire safety exits, and Infection Control Parameters. Our designs ensure proper zoning (Clean, Sterile, and Dirty areas) to prevent cross-contamination.
What is the importance of Laminar Airflow in your OT designs?
Laminar Airflow is critical for ultra-clean surgical environments. We install systems that provide a continuous flow of HEPA-filtered air moving in a single direction. This "washes" the surgical site, significantly reducing the risk of surgical site infections (SSI) by pushing airborne contaminants away from the patient.
Do you handle Medical Gas Pipeline System (MGPS) certification?
Yes. We design and install MGPS according to HTM 02-01 standards. This includes the central plant, liquid oxygen tanks, and manifold systems. Every installation undergoes rigorous pressure testing and purity analysis before being handed over to the hospital clinical team.
How do you manage infrastructure work in a functional hospital?
We specialize in "Live Facility Integration." We use dust-containment barriers and noise-reduction schedules to ensure that patient care is not disrupted. Our team works in shifts—often during night hours—to complete heavy installations without affecting the hospital's daily operations.
What are the specific requirements for Radiology Room shielding?
For X-ray, CT Scan, and Cath Labs, we provide Lead Shielding (Atomic No. 82) of specific thicknesses calculated by our radiation safety experts. For MRI rooms, we construct a Faraday Cage using high-purity copper or aluminum to block external Radio Frequency (RF) interference, ensuring crystal-clear diagnostic images.
